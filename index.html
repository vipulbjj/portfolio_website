Principles

Beliefs I try to keep even when the company changes shape.

Operating principles (7)
  • Uncertainty is the point. Markets, models, and minds are interesting because they refuse to guarantee outcomes.
  • Claims need friction. A hypothesis earns trust only after data, users, or a market pushes back.
  • Companies are experiments. Startups let me run studies papers cannot. The product may pivot. The method should not.
  • Shipping counts. From HFT to production ML: if it is not built and measured, the research is not done.
  • Structure before scale. LLMs help when memory and synthesis exist. They hurt when prompts stand in for thinking.
  • Think in decades. Health, reputation, and knowledge compound. A quarter is too short a backtest for a life or a field.
  • Write what failed. The weekly posts include dead ends, not just wins.
